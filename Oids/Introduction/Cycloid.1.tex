
 \Example[Cycloids]{
 Rolling a circle of ratio $r$ on the $x$-axis, results in a curve called a Cycloid,
 parameterized by:
 
 \[
 \Vector{r}(t)
 =
  \begin{pmatrix}
   x(t)\\y(t)
  \end{pmatrix}
  =
  \begin{pmatrix}
   a(t-\cos{t})\\a(1- \sin{t})
  \end{pmatrix},
  \qquad
  t \in [-\pi,\pi]
 \]
 
 Convinientely, we write:
 
 \[
 \Vector{r}(t)
 =
   t
   \begin{pmatrix}
   a\\-1
  \end{pmatrix}
  -
  a \Vector{e}
 \]

 Deriving once:
 
 \[
   \Vector{r}'(t)
 =
   a 
   (
   \Vector{i}
    -
    \Vector{f}
   )
 \]
 We observe, that the Cycloid is regular ($\Vector{r}'(t) \neq \Vector{0}$), 
 in all points, except:
 
 \[
  t=\frac{\pi}{2} +2 p \pi, \quad p \in \mathbb{Z}
 \]
 
 For completeness:
 
 \[
  \Vector{r}''(t)
 =
 a \Vector{e}
 \]
 }
